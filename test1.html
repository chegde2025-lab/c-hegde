Vehicle Insurance Terminology Explained

1. Policy

A legal contract between the insured and the insurance company.

Example: Michael Johnson buys auto insurance for $2,400 per year with $100,000 liability coverage.

2. Premium

The amount paid to the insurance company for coverage.

Example: $200 per month = $2,400 annual premium.

3. Exposure

Exposure measures how much risk the insurer is covering over time.

  • 1 vehicle insured for 1 year = 1 vehicle-year exposure
  • Insured for 6 months = 0.5 exposure
Frequency = Claims / Exposure
Example: 10 claims for 1,000 vehicles â†’ 1% claim frequency.

4. Coverage Limit

The maximum amount the insurer will pay for a covered loss.

Example: $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident.

5. Deductible

The amount the insured pays before insurance pays.

Repair cost = $12,000
Deductible = $1,000
Insurance pays = $11,000

6. Claim

A request for payment after a covered accident or loss.

Michael rear-ends another vehicle and files a claim.

7. Claim Lifecycle

  1. FNOL (First Notice of Loss)
  2. Coverage Verification
  3. Investigation
  4. Reserve Setup
  5. Payment
  6. Settlement
  7. Closure

8. Reserve

Money set aside by the insurer to pay future claim costs.

Estimated loss: $80,000
Legal cost reserve: $10,000
Total reserve: $90,000

9. Total Incurred

Total claim cost including paid and outstanding reserve.

Total Incurred = Paid Amount + Outstanding Reserve

10. Indemnity

Compensation paid to restore financial position (no profit).

11. Actual Cash Value (ACV)

Replacement cost minus depreciation.

Car worth new: $30,000
Current value: $20,000
ACV payout = $20,000

12. Replacement Cost (RCV)

Cost to replace without deducting depreciation.

13. Subrogation

Insurer recovers money from the responsible third party after paying claim.

Insurance pays $25,000 and later recovers $10,000 from the at-fault driver.

14. Salvage

Value of damaged vehicle after total loss.

Insurance pays $18,000.
Salvage value = $3,000.
Net loss = $15,000.

15. Loss Ratio

Loss Ratio = Claims / Earned Premium
Claims = $5,000,000
Premium = $8,000,000
Loss Ratio = 62.5%

16. Combined Ratio

Combined Ratio = Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio

If below 100%, insurer makes underwriting profit.

17. Unearned Premium

Premium for future coverage period.

If policy canceled after 3 months, unused 9 months premium is refunded.

18. Large Loss Example

Medical: $120,000
Vehicle damage: $40,000
Legal cost: $20,000
Total claim: $180,000

19. Reinsurance

Insurance for insurance companies to protect against large losses.

20. Key Insurance Metrics

  • Claim Frequency
  • Claim Severity
  • Loss Ratio
  • Reserve Development
  • Subrogation Recovery Rate

Conclusion

Vehicle insurance works by pooling risk across many exposures. Individual claims may be large, but insurers rely on proper pricing, reserving, underwriting, and reinsurance to remain financially stable.
