Vehicle Insurance Terminology & Process Guide

1. Exposure → Premium → Claims Relationship

Exposure
(Vehicle-Year)
→
Claim Frequency
(Claims / Exposure)
→
Claim Severity
(Avg $ per Claim)
→
Expected Loss
→
Premium
Premium ≈ Frequency × Severity + Expenses + Profit Margin

Example: 1,000 vehicles insured for 1 year = 1,000 exposures. If 20 claims occur → Frequency = 2%. Average claim cost = $15,000 → Expected loss drives premium pricing.

2. Claim Lifecycle Flowchart

FNOL
(First Notice of Loss)
→
Coverage
Verification
→
Investigation
→
Liability
Decision
Reserve
Setup
→
Payment
→
Negotiation / Litigation
→
Subrogation
→
Claim Closure

Example: Michael files a claim after accident → Reserve set at $90,000 → Payments made → Final settlement $140,000 → $25,000 recovery → Net loss $115,000.

3. Claim Financial Structure

Total Incurred
=
Paid Amount
+
Outstanding Reserve
If Paid = $60,000 and Reserve = $40,000 Total Incurred = $100,000

4. Reserve Development Flow

Initial Reserve
$50,000
→
Medical Worsens
Increase to $120,000
→
Settlement
$110,000
→
Release Extra Reserve

Reserve increases or decreases over time based on new information. This change is called Reserve Development.

5. Loss Ratio & Combined Ratio Diagram

Earned Premium
→
Claims (Losses)
→
Loss Ratio
Loss Ratio = Claims / Earned Premium Combined Ratio = Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio If Combined Ratio < 100% → Underwriting Profit

6. Large Loss Impact Diagram

Many Small Premiums
$2,400 × 1,000
→
Risk Pool
→
Few Large Claims
$500,000+

Insurance works by pooling many exposures to absorb large but infrequent losses.

Conclusion

Vehicle insurance combines underwriting, exposure measurement, claim management, reserving, and financial monitoring to manage risk effectively. Understanding the flow between exposure → premium → claims → reserves is essential for mastering insurance fundamentals.
